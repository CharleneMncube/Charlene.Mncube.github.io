Intro

Charlene is a Civil Engineer by training with a demonstrated background in problem-solving, data management, and programming. She holds a degree in Civil Engineering from the University of Johannesburg (UJ), and a National Certificate in IT Systems Development from the Explore AI Academy.

With over four years of experience working in the engineering consulting industry, primarily on transportation projects at the Global Design Centre, she has had the opportunity to work on a diverse range of world-class transportation projects in collaboration with colleagues from Australia and New Zealand. Throughout this experience, she has been responsible for various aspects of project management, designing, modelling, drafting, project finance, and project reporting on major infrastructure projects.

Charlene has a keen interest in developing innovative solutions and strategies to tackle complex business problems, with a specific focus on using digital and analytics transformation to support sustainable infrastructure development.

Her passion for engineering innovation inspired her final year research project, which assessed the Fourth Industrial Revolution's impact on the Civil Engineering industry in South Africa. This project further motivated her to pursue personal and knowledge acquisition related to innovation adoption and implementation particularly within the engineering and technology industries and it’s impacts on African communities.

Alongside her technical proficiency, Charlene possesses strong interpersonal skills as she often engages with people from diverse backgrounds, whether in a boardroom with executives or at a braai with colleagues. Those close to her would describe Charlene as conscientious, witty, sociable, self-aware, ambitious, empathetic, and candid.

Impact of the Fourth Industrial Revolution on the Civil Engineering Industry Dissertation

| 2019

Like many civil engineers, my career aspiration was to improve communities and the economy by being the brains behind civil infrastructure design. However, the reality of the role of a civil engineer in our country was underwhelming compared to the picture I had in my mind. In my third year, a presentation by Dr. Gabrielle Wojtowitz from a top engineering consulting company on the use of AI and virtual reality in their engineering projects sparked my interest in the synergy between engineering and technology. For my final year dissertation, I researched the impacts of the 4th Industrial Revolution on the Civil Engineering Industry, which allowed me to network with like-minded industry professionals and learn about emerging technologies and disruptors. What excited me the most about my studies was the potential value propositions emerging tech had for the engineering industry. I believed that I could be a voice and contributor to the change and bring my ideas to life someday.

Safe@Home Hackathon Team Member

| 2020
During the COVID lockdown, I was ideating on opportunities where I could apply my skills for a positive impact and came across the 'Safe @ Home Hackathon - Send Assist Victim in Danger (S.A.V.I.D)' challenge sponsored by Microsoft. This challenge aligned perfectly with my passion for social-tech. I and a few curious minds decided to create a tech driven solution, which we later named UNCEDO Reliable Relief Powered by Technology, which aimed to develop an affordable technology solution to address gender-based violence in South Africa. The solution had to cater to victims using older phones without transport means, identify hotspots, recognize at-risk individuals, profile perpetrators, and offer alert systems for educators, social workers, and the community. We focused on making our solution economical and accessible to rural communities.

Civil Engineering and and Beyond

| 2019 - Present
I am now well within 4 years of my career as an engineer, and I've noticed that not much of the innovation I envisioned has been effectively implemented locally in the industry yet. However, the industry is steadily starting to recognize the importance of optimizing their business with technology. Currently, I work in the offshoring unit on transportation projects in Australia and New Zealand, which has provided an incredible opportunity to collaborate with some of the industry's best engineering minds. Nevertheless, the disconnect between working on first-world projects while living in a developing country emphasizes the discrepancy and highlights how much further we need to go as a continent to achieve world-class sustainable and innovative infrastructure. Though we use sophisticated engineering software, there is still room for more efficient solutions both on-site and in the office. Last year, I presented the digital twin model for our company's roads learning week and realized my interest lies in unearthing innovative solutions to solve complex business problems. As an engineer, I have acquired invaluable skills and knowledge, which makes me an intrinsic problem solver. Unfortunately, engineers are undervalued in South Africa, leading to the current brain drain. The industry needs adequate funding and support from an innovation fund to fully thrive. As a young professional, I will continue to adapt and evolve with the disruptions and apply my diverse skills to make a global impact.
